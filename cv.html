Research

Amino Functional Group Based Prophylactic Antibiotic Loaded Hydrogel for Surgical Site Infection in Spine Surgery

Collaborator: Dr. Jahid Ferdous

Surgical site infections (SSI) are one of the most prevalent complications following spinal surgery. Following spine surgery, SSIs can be superficial, with visible wound drainage, or deep-seated, with a healed wound. The predominant causative agent remains Staphylococcus aureus. Preoperative and postoperative antibiotic prophylaxis is preferred to prevent these infections. Antibiotics taken systemically have limited antibacterial activity and must be given at regular intervals for successful treatment. However, this could result in overdose and the emergence of antimicrobial resistance. Injectable, in situ gel-forming systems like hydrogels are threedimensional, cross-linked polymer networks that can contain and distribute potential therapeutic agents like cells, medicines, and growth hormones directly at the target site. Covalently loaded drugs in hydrogels can be delivered in a controlled manner over time, eliminating the need to administer antibiotics at regular intervals. PDA, a bioadhesive polymer, and chitosan, an inherently antibacterial polymer polysaccharide were used to make the hydrogels in this study. Antibiotics containing a free primary amine group react completely or partially with the gel matrix via imine bond formation, depending on the drug/PDA ratio. To be encapsulated in the hydrogels, three distinct antibiotics (cefotaxime, vancomycin, and ampicillin) from the groups of most common preventive antibiotics for Surgical Site infections in Spinal Surgery were chosen. The hydrogels were subjected to a comparative drug release analysis. After an initial burst release, the drugs continued to release antibiotics for more than 72 hours, according to the study. Because the drug release is largely regulated by the opening of covalent imine bonds, the sustained release after the burst release was almost linear. It can also be observed from the comparative study that cefotaxime showed the highest release rate with 13.48% release over 72h. These antibiotic-loaded hydrogels have a great potentiality as a drug delivery medium treating surgical site infections after spine surgery since they showed significant release over an extended period of time without requiring continuous medication administration.

Projects

  • Developing a Web Application for digitalization of ICU Communication system

    • Our task for this project was to visit multiple Critical Care Units in various hospitals and identify existing problems and devise an engineering solution. After visiting the ICUs in the major hospitals in Dhaka city, we identified the need for a complete communication system for ICUs. To meet this need, we built a web application using Vue JS, HTML, CSS and Firebase. This web app featured integrated database, communication channels and different, easy-to-use user interfaces for the patients’ attendants and the medical service providers.
